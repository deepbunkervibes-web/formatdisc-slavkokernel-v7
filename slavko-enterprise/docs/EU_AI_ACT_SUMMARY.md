# Introduction to the EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689)

**Status:** Force as of Aug 1, 2024.
**Goal:** Ensure safe, transparent, traceable, non-discriminatory, and sustainable AI.

## 1. Scope and Definitions

- **Applies to:** Providers/deployers in EU, or output used in EU.
- **Key Definitions:**
  - **AI System:** Machine-based, autonomous, infers outputs.
  - **GPAI Model:** General-purpose, large datasets, diverse tasks.
  - **Systemic Risk:** >10^25 FLOPs training.

## 2. Risk-Based Approach (4 Levels)

1. **Unacceptable Risk (Prohibited):** Social scoring, subliminal manipulation, real-time remote biometric scraping (public).
2. **High-Risk (Strict Obligations):** Critical infra, education, employment, law enforcement, medical devices. Requires: Risk management, data governance, human oversight, logging.
3. **Limited Risk (Transparency):** Chatbots, emotional recognition, deepfakes. Must disclose AI interaction/labeling.
4. **Minimal Risk:** Spam filters, games. No mandatory rules.

## 3. Obligations

- **Providers:** Primary responsibility. Risk assessments, QMS, documentation.
- **GPAI Providers:** Tech docs, copyright policy, training summaries. Systemic GPAI needs adversarial testing & cyber-security.
- **Deployers:** Monitor usage, human oversight, complaint handling.

## 4. Enforcement & Penalties

- **Bodies:** EU AI Office (GPAI), AI Board, Scientific Panel, National Authorities.
- **Penalties:**
  - **€35M / 7% turnover:** Prohibited practices.
  - **€15M / 3% turnover:** Other obligations (GPAI).
  - **€7.5M / 1% turnover:** Misleading info.

## 5. Timeline (2025 Updates)

- **Feb 2025:** Prohibitions active.
- **May 2025:** GPAI Codes of Practice.
- **Aug 2025:** GPAI obligations start (docs, copyright). AI Office operational. Penalties active.
- **Aug 2026/2027:** High-risk obligations phased in.
